Abstract

Cooling infrastructures in HPC environments generate large volumes of heterogeneous telemetry that require coherent semantic representation for effective monitoring and analysis. The HPCC ontology provides this foundation by formally modeling HPC cooling systems, including platforms, sensors, properties, and observations, in alignment with W3C SSN/SOSA standards. It captures structural and operational relationships across the cooling stack, enabling consistent interpretation of sensor data and system behavior. Integrated within the HPCCool framework, the ontology supports ontology based data access, allowing queries to operate directly over live telemetry without centralized storage. Real world deployments demonstrate how the ontology enables semantic enrichment, anomaly detection, and advanced analytics across HPC cooling infrastructures. The ontology has been evaluated for logical consistency, domain coverage, and operational applicability, establishing it as a reusable semantic asset for HPC and broader data engineering domains.
